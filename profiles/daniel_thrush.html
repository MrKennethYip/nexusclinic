Tell us about yourself?

Daniel is Registered Acupuncturist and Certified Nutritional Practitioner, and a good standing member of the College of Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioners and Acupuncturists of Ontario (CTCMPAO). As a teen, he became interested in weight training, and learned early on about the importance of a healthy-balanced lifestyle. He soon realized that to fully optimize his athletic ability, not only exercise but also his nutrition, lifestyle, and mental outlook had to be addressed, which he found also had the double benefit of improving many other areas of his life. With this realization, his interests soon became his passion and the catalyst that led him to his studies and career path.

He attended the Institute of Holistic Nutrition and graduated in 2011. His dedication for exercise eventually led him into fitness competitions where he placed 1st in the Men’s Physique category in 2012 with the Ontario Physique Association. With his awareness of natural health continuing to grow, he became fascinated with the medical system of Traditional Chinese Medicine, which then brought him to study and earn his diploma at the Toronto School of Traditional Chinese Medicine in 2015.

Which cases do you enjoy treating? Why?

Anxiety and mental health related conditions. This one really hits home for me. When I am able to see my patient gradually regain that sensation of comfort and reassurance within their body and mind, it is something that I find very satisfying in my line of work.

What are the top 3 treatments that you specialize in?

Pain, digestive issues and mental health/sleep.

What are you proud of in your practice?

I am proud that I am able to make a person's life just a little bit easier by helping them with their health concerns that they have been dealing with, hopefully leaving them feeling better so they are more able to fully enjoy their life.

Why should patients choose you as their provider instead of others in the area?

I genuinely care about them and helping people is why I got into this industry. I see no greater reward in a career than being able to help someone feel better.
