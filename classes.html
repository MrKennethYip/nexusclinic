Why Dance?

Dancing has been proven to be one of the most beneficial exercises for physical and mental well-being. Research has indicated that it can ward off the onset of Alzheimer's due to the creative process involved in dancing. In the process of learning dance, you will develop good balance, posture, coordination and speed. This is also a ton of fun and a great way to make new friends!

Salsa and Cha-Cha-Cha Classes!

Instructor: Teddy Olaso from UnitedSalseros

Salsa & Technique On2

Level Difficulty
Lvl 0-1 Intro - Beginner

Schedule:

Monday (8:45pm - 9:40pm)

Description:

This level is designed for those with little or no salsa experience (around 2-5 lessons). The students are introduced to most fundamental footwork and basic lead and follow technique.

Students will also be introduced to some of the key steps in salsa dancing and are required to attain a functional level in timing, chorography and technique to advance to the next level.

Salsa & Technique On2

Level Difficulty
Lvl 4 Intermediate - Advanced

Schedule:

Monday (9:45pm - 10:40pm)

Description:

This level uses the 10 Salsa Key Steps and their variations to create constantly changing choreography on a weekly basis while technique and timing in all areas of movements are stressed at a much higher level.

Solo and partner-work styling (arm work and body isolation) and shines (fancy footwork) are also covered in this class to give students a comprehensive repertoire of figures to work with.
